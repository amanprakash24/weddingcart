- Acts of God, earthquake, flood, fire, storm, or natural disaster;
- Epidemic, pandemic, or government-declared public health emergency;
- War, civil unrest, riot, or terrorist activity;
- Government orders, prohibitory orders, Section 144 CrPC impositions, curfew, or official lockdowns;
- Nationwide or regional bandhs, strikes affecting essential services;
- Grid failure, major infrastructure breakdown.

6.2 The Party invoking Force Majeure shall notify the other Party in writing within **48 (forty-eight) hours** of the occurrence of the event.

6.3 In the event of a Force Majeure affecting the event date, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to reschedule. If rescheduling is not possible, amounts paid (excluding the non-refundable advance) shall be refunded within **30 (thirty) days**.
